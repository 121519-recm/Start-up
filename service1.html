How E-learning works?

Synchronous
e-Learning

Real-time learning with instructors and peers online.

Asynchronous
e-Learning

Self-paced learning through pre-recorded lectures, videos, and other materials.

Blended
e-Learning

A combination of online and face-to-face instruction.

Adaptive
e-Learning

Customized learning paths based on individual progress and abilities.

Gamification
e-Learning

Using game elements to engage learners and promote learning.

Mobile
e-Learning

Learning on-the-go through mobile devices such as smartphones and tablets.

These are software platforms that help in managing, delivering, and tracking online courses, providing learners with access to course content, assessments, and communication tools.

These tools include audio, video, graphics, and animations, which can be used to create engaging and interactive e-learning content that captures learners' attention and facilitates better learning outcomes.

These portable devices, such as smartphones and tablets, provide learners with access to e-learning content anytime and anywhere, enabling them to learn on-the-go, which is particularly beneficial for learners who have limited time or are unable to attend traditional classroom settings.
